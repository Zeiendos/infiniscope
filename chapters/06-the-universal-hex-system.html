INFİNIOLOGY — CHAPTER XI The Universal Hex System A Cross-Disciplinary Language for Modeling Constraint, Interaction, and Failure I. PURPOSE OF THIS CHAPTER

This chapter defines how and why the hex system works across all fields of study.

The hexes are not metaphors, symbols, or numerology.
They are constraint triplets — minimal models describing:

what can change

what resists change

how systems break, adapt, or stabilize

Any system that evolves under limitation can be expressed as a hex.

This includes physics, biology, psychology, computation, sociology, and meaning-making itself.

II. WHAT A HEX IS (CANONICAL DEFINITION)

A hex is a three-axis constraint signature.

#AABBCC

Each paired element represents:

a degree of freedom

a limitation

an interaction channel

The pairing indicates balance, not hierarchy.

III. WHY THREE AXES (AND NOT MORE)

Every real system, regardless of domain, must answer three questions:

What is being acted upon?

What enables or resists change?

What governs persistence over time?

These collapse naturally into triads.

Across domains, these recur as:

Force / Medium / Time

Signal / Noise / Refresh

Desire / Control / Meaning

Entropy / Gravity / Information

The hex system captures this irreducible minimum.
