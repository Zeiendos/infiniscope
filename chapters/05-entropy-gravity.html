INFİNIOLOGY — CHAPTER: ENTROPY & GRAVITY Two Meta-Pressures That Shape All Structure

I. PURPOSE This chapter defines the two highest-level pressures acting on every system: entropy (dispersion) and gravity (aggregation / coherence).

II. DEFINITIONS (CANON) Entropy (#AASSTT): the pressure toward spreading, noise, and probability flattening over time. Gravity (#1122DD): the pressure toward aggregation, binding, and local coherence.

III. WHY THIS MATTERS Every stable pattern is a local win against entropy — temporarily. Systems fail when refresh, coherence, or authority cannot keep pace with entropy pressure.

IV. PRACTICAL READ - If entropy dominates: decay, fragmentation, noise, forgetting. - If gravity dominates: rigidity, over-binding, capture, stagnation. - Health is not “maximum gravity.” Health is regulated balance.

V. LINK BACK TO HEX SPACE Entropy increases LOAD and attacks PERSISTENCE. Gravity increases COHERENCE but can become RIGIDITY if unchecked.

VI. ANCHOR SENTENCE Structure exists where gravity locally defeats entropy — until it can’t.
