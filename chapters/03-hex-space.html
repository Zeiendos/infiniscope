INFİNIOLOGY — CHAPTER: HEX SPACE Load • Coherence • Persistence (the diagnostic core)

I. PURPOSE This chapter gives the universal diagnostic language used everywhere in Infiniology.

II. THE THREE AXES LOAD: the pressure entering a system (demand, energy, stress, input). COHERENCE: the structure that shapes flow (alignment, constraint, control). PERSISTENCE: what endures over time and transition (refresh, memory, maintenance).

III. SYSTEM STATES STABLE: load is met, coherence holds, persistence is protected. STRAINED: progress is possible but costly; debt accumulates. FLOW: high coherence with sustainable load and positive reinforcement.

IV. FAILURE MODES OVERLOAD: load exceeds coherence/refresh. RIGIDITY: coherence over-binds; adaptation stalls. DECAY: persistence fails; capability erodes over time.

V. THE UNIVERSAL QUESTION When systems fail, don’t ask “what’s wrong?” Ask: where is load too high, coherence too low, or persistence unprotected — and who has authority to change it?
