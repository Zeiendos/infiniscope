INFİNIOLOGY — CHAPTER XII Authority, Control, & Power How Order Is Enforced Against Entropy I. PURPOSE OF THIS CHAPTER

This chapter defines authority not as morality, status, or force, but as a system function:

Authority is the capacity to decide which patterns persist.

Power is not violence. Control is not oppression. Authority is selection under constraint.

This chapter explains:

why systems need authority

how power actually operates

when authority stabilizes vs corrupts

II. CORE DEFINITIONS (LOCK THESE) Authority

The ability to set or enforce constraints.

Control

The mechanism by which authority acts on flow.

Power

The energy or capacity available to enforce control.

These are distinct. Most confusion comes from mixing them.

III. THE AUTHORITY HEX (CANONICAL) Authority & Control

#SSFFPP

SS — Scope What domain authority applies to (self, body, group, system, society)

FF — Force How authority is enforced (coercion, incentive, structure, habit)

PP — Persistence How long authority maintains effect (temporary, habitual, institutional)

This hex applies universally.

IV. HOW AUTHORITY OPERATES ACROSS FIELDS Physics

Gravity is authority over mass

Laws are constraints, not choices

Biology

Homeostasis is authority over chemistry

DNA is authority over form

Psychology

Attention is authority over thought

Identity is authority over behavior

Computation

Scheduler is authority over processes

Permissions are authority over access

Society

Laws are authority over action

Culture is authority over meaning

Authority always answers:

“What is allowed to continue?”

ConceptMeaning
PowerCapacity to act
AuthorityRight/structure to decide
ControlExecution of authority

A system can:

have power without authority (chaos, abuse)

have authority without power (collapse)

require both to remain stable

VI. FAILURE MODES OF AUTHORITY

Authority collapses when:

Scope exceeds capacity → tyranny

Force exceeds legitimacy → rebellion

Persistence exceeds adaptability → stagnation

In Infiniology terms:

Authority fails when gravity overbinds and entropy retaliates.

VII. AUTHORITY & THE OBSERVER

At the individual level:

Attention = authority over perception

Belief = authority over meaning

Habit = authority over behavior

Loss of authority → fragmentation Excess authority → rigidity

Mental health is adaptive authority balance.
