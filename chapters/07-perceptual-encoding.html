II. THE CORE AXIOMS (LOCK THESE) Axiom 1 — Color is Perceptual Encoding

Color does not exist inside the brain as light. Color is how the mind encodes internal state.

Axiom 2 — Three Axes Govern All Perceptual States

Every perceptual/emotional state can be described by:

HUE → Cognitive Mode (what kind of processing) SATURATION → Coherence (order vs noise) BRIGHTNESS → Amplitude (intensity / energy)

This replaces vague “high vs low vibration” language with measurable structure.

APPENDIX — COLOR + SOUND + Hz (Bias, Not Mysticism) (This appendix anchors the color-psychology chart and adds audio frequency bands.)

1) The Core Claim

Color and sound do not contain meaning.

They bias:

what gets prioritized (visual salience)

when change is expected (temporal expectation)

how strongly signals are felt (emotional gain)

Meaning is assigned after these biases act.

2) The Three-Layer Chain (No Skips) 2.1 Perception → Regulation → Experience Stimulus (light/sound) → transduction (eyes/ears/nervous system) → regulation (arousal, stress response, attention control) → experience (emotion + thought + interpretation) → meaning (narrative assigned last)

2.2 Why this matters

Two people can hear the same sound or see the same color and report different feelings because:

regulation differs

context differs

prior associations differ

So we model bias, not universal “truth”.

3) The Encoding Framework (Infiniology-Compatible)

This chapter uses three compatible “mini-hexes” as one cross-lens:

3.1 Color Encoding (Visual Priority) HEX_COLOR: #HHSSBB

HH — Hue category (what kind of situation it “feels like”) SS — Saturation / gain (emotional signal strength) BB — Brightness / weight (attention allocation)

3.2 Music / Sound Encoding (Temporal Expectation) HEX_MUSIC: #RRTTHHDD

RR — Rhythm (temporal grid) TT — Tempo (update rate) HH — Harmony (stability / grounding) DD — Dissonance (entropy injection / uncertainty)

3.3 Emotional Regulation (Mediator Layer) HEX_EMOTION: #AARRCC

AA — Arousal RR — Regulation capacity CC — Coherence (organized feeling/thought)

Rule:
Color shapes WHAT becomes salient.
Sound shapes WHEN change is expected.
Emotion determines HOW strongly the system reacts.

5) Sound Frequency (Hz) as Bias—What It Actually Does 5.1 The non-mystical definition

Hz describes the rate of vibration in a sound wave (pitch frequency).
In audio perception, frequency bands correlate with:

felt body weight (low frequencies)

clarity and presence (mid to upper-mid)

brightness/air (high frequencies)

Frequency alone does not determine emotion, but it strongly influences:

arousal

tension

fatigue

perceived “brightness” or “darkness” of sound

6) How Color and Sound Influence Each Other (Without Synesthesia Claims)

They meet in the shared mediator: attention + arousal.

6.1 Functional pairing rule

Color adjusts visual salience (what stands out)

Frequency content adjusts auditory salience (what cuts through)

Together they determine overall render priority

6.2 Practical pairing tendencies (bias-level)

These are useful for design, therapy environments, and art direction:

Urgency / push

Visual: red/orange, high brightness

Sound: 2k–5k presence + faster tempo → boosts alertness and action bias → overload risk: agitation

Stability / recovery

Visual: green/blue, moderate brightness, lower saturation

Sound: 60–250 warmth + stable harmony + moderate/slow tempo → supports regulation and downshift → overload risk: dullness or emotional flattening

Novelty / excitement

Visual: yellow/orange, higher saturation

Sound: brighter highs + rhythmic variation → increases exploration bias → overload risk: restlessness

Depth / contemplation

Visual: purple/blue, lower brightness, controlled saturation

Sound: low-mid warmth + gentle highs + slow tempo + resolution → supports reflective coherence → overload risk: heaviness if too dark

Compression / dominance

Visual: black/low brightness

Sound: strong sub/bass + limited high detail → can feel powerful, contained → overload risk: oppression, negativity

7) Where Emotion Actually Enters (The Mediator Hex)

Emotion is not “caused by color” or “caused by Hz.” Emotion emerges when the system evaluates salience + expectation under regulation.

7.1 A simple regulation reading

If AA (arousal) rises but RR (regulation) is strong → energized, focused

If AA rises and RR is weak → anxious, irritable, overwhelmed

If AA drops too low for too long → numbness, apathy

7.2 Common failure patterns

High saturation + high brightness + high presence band (2–5k) → salience overload (overwhelm, irritability)

Dark visuals + heavy low mids + unresolved dissonance → gravity trap (sadness, rumination)

High tempo + no harmonic resolution → open loop stress (anxiety, fixation)

8) The “Hz Myth” Correction (Required Safety Section) 8.1 What’s true

Frequency bands influence arousal, tension, and fatigue.

Rhythm and harmonic resolution strongly shape expectation and relief.

These factors can influence stress physiology indirectly (breathing, posture, vigilance).

8.2 What is not supported as a universal claim

“This exact frequency always heals X.”

“This tone aligns your body to a fixed emotional state.”

“This frequency changes atoms or chemistry directly.”

Infiniology framing:
Color and sound bias render configuration.
They do not override constraints.

9) How to Use This (Professional, Non-Manipulative) 9.1 For environment design (workspaces, clinics, classrooms)

Decide the target state: calm focus, social warmth, deep work, recovery

Choose color bias (HH) and moderate gain (SS/BB)

Choose sound bias via tempo + frequency balance

Watch for overload signals and reduce gain

9.2 For therapy/coaching settings (non-clinical)

Use as a reflection tool:

“What feels loud right now—visual or auditory?”

“Is your system in urgency-bias or stability-bias?”

“Do you feel trapped in open-loop tension (no resolution)?”

Do not use as diagnosis. Do not prescribe “frequencies.”

9.3 For artists/creators

Treat it like composition:

color = scene priority

sound = time pressure

emotion = the mediator

meaning = what the audience assigns last
